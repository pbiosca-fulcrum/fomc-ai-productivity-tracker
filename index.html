For Markets

What This Means for Rates

  • The productivity-disinflation link "several" participants now endorse is a DOVISH signal
  • Higher potential output = economy can run hotter without generating inflation
  • BUT: energy/capex/debt channels create near-term upward pressure on prices
  • Net: AI is being incorporated as a reason rates can stay LOWER FOR LONGER
  • Not yet a reason to cut — but removing a reason to hike
"Several participants expected higher productivity growth... to put downward pressure on inflation" — Jan '26 FOMC Minutes  Source

8 Takeaways for IC

  1. AI is a first-order policy variable — 38% of all communications mention it
  2. Productivity attribution debate is live but tilting — chair's language stronger than median
  3. Minutes language escalation: "a few" → "several" on productivity-disinflation
  4. Labor market signals hardening — sandbox to production, entry-level suppression
  5. Financial stability as counterweight — >$1T debt, concentration risk
  6. Waller's evolution most dramatic — "novelty party gag" to "never seen a revolution like this"
  7. Beige Book catching up — 5/8 entries now mention AI/automation
  8. For rates: dovish signal, but not yet a cut trigger
